About Our History

Our lead game designer and technician, Brendan Greene, also known as PlayerUnknown online, first created a mod for ARMA 2 called DayZ: Battle Royale, inspired by the popular Japanese movie and novel Battle Royale.

Separately, the Seoul-based studio Ginno Games, led by Chang-han Kim, was acquired and renamed Bluehole Ginno Games by Bluehole in January 2015, a major South Korean publisher of MMOs and mobile games. Around the same time, Kim contacted and offered Greene the opportunity to work on a new battle royale concept. Within a week, Greene flew out to Bluehole's headquarters in Korea to discuss the options, and a few weeks later, became the creative director of Bluehole. He moved to South Korea to oversee development. Development began in early 2016 and was publicly announced that June, with plans to have the game ready within a year. Kim served as executive producer for the game.

Brendan Greene

Designer

Brendan Greene, better known by his online handle PlayerUnknown, is the lead designer and director of PlayerUnknown's Battlegrounds. He had previously worked on other games using the 2000 film Battle Royale for inspiration, and expanded into a standalone game under Greene's creative direction.

Tom Salta

Composer

Tom Salta is an American recording artist and soundtrack composer. He has composed soundtracks for many video game titles, including Cold Fear, Red Steel and PUBG. Salta's score for Red Steel received an award for "Best Original Score" in IGN's Best of 2006 awards.
